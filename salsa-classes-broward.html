Timing & Styles

Understanding Salsa styles: On1 vs. On2.

At Fajardo Elite, we teach both major styles of Salsa. Each has its own distinct timing, rhythm, and styling, and knowing both will make you a highly versatile social dancer.

Salsa On1 (LA Style)

Linear & Dynamic

Salsa On1, commonly known as LA Style, breaks forward on the first count of the music (the "1" beat). It is highly energetic, linear, and visual.

  • Timing: Emphasis is placed on the first and fifth beats of the measure.
  • Vibe: Fast-paced, theatrical, with expressive turns, spins, and styling.
  • Who it's for: Fantastic for beginners looking to catch the beat quickly and build basic confidence.
Salsa On2 (Mambo / NY Style)

Rhythmic & Connected

Salsa On2, historically referred to as Mambo or New York Style, breaks on the second beat of the measure (the "2" count). It is deeply connected to the percussion.

  • Timing: The primary break step happens on the 2 and 6 beats, aligned with the Conga "slap."
  • Vibe: Smooth, rhythmic, with a heavier focus on instrumentals, timing, and dance partner connection.
  • Who it's for: Popular at social dance clubs and congresses worldwide. A favorite for intermediate and advanced dancers.

Find Your Level

Salsa class levels.

We structure our Salsa classes to ensure everyone learns in a comfortable environment that matches their pace.

  • Level 1 — Introduction to Basics: Designed for absolute beginners. We cover the basic steps, timing, turns, and lead-and-follow foundation. No experience required.
  • Level 2 — Advanced Beginner: For students who know their basics. We introduce cross-body leads, left turns, simple partner patterns, and beginner shines (footwork).
  • Level 3 — Intermediate & Advanced: Focused on complex turn patterns, timing shifts, advanced shines, and body styling to help you stand out on the social dance floor.
  • Ladies Styling Team: An audition-only performance team that focuses on styling, choreography, stage presence, and synchronization. Perfect for dedicated dancers looking to push their limits.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need a partner to join a Salsa class?

No partner is required! In our group classes, we rotate partners frequently. This helps you build lead/follow versatility and helps you meet other dancers in the studio.

What should I wear to my first class?

Wear comfortable clothing that lets you move freely. For shoes, we recommend smooth leather or suede-soled shoes that slide easily. Avoid sticky running shoes or boots, which can strain your knees during turns.

How long does it take to learn Salsa?

Most students can learn the basic rhythm and simple patterns in 4 to 6 weeks. With 3 to 6 months of weekly classes and practice, you will feel confident social dancing with any partner at a club.

Can I just drop in, or do I need to register ahead of time?

You can drop in to any class! A single drop-in is $20. You can also sign up for our weekly memberships if you plan to attend regularly, which drops the per-class rate significantly.
